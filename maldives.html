Maldives

The Maldives is an island nation in the Indian Ocean, made up of a chain of coral islands: Location: The Maldives is located in two rows of atolls, southwest of the Indian subcontinent. Geography: The Maldives is made up of 1,190 coral islands, with most being uninhabited. The islands are ring-shaped atolls, with each atoll made up of a coral reef surrounding a lagoon. Climate: The Maldives has an equatorial climate, with two distinct monsoon seasons. Economy: Tourism is the main industry in the Maldives, with many islands developed for high-end tourists. Vulnerability: The Maldives is vulnerable to rising sea levels, as none of the islands are more than 1.8 meters above sea level. Biodiversity: The Maldives has some of the richest marine biodiversity in the world. History: In the 1800s, the Maldives used cowrie shells as a currency for international trading. The shells were distinctive, lightweight, and could not be forged.
