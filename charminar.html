Charminar

The Charminar is an iconic monument located in Hyderabad, India. Built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the fifth ruler of the Qutb Shahi dynasty, it was erected to commemorate the founding of Hyderabad and to mark the end of a deadly plague in the region. The name "Charminar" comes from the Urdu words "Char," meaning four, and "Minar," meaning towers or minarets, as the structure is characterized by four grand minarets on its corners. This majestic structure is a perfect square, with each side measuring about 20 meters (66 feet). The four minarets rise to a height of nearly 50 meters (160 feet). Built in Indo-Islamic architectural style with Persian influences, the Charminar features intricate stucco decorations, arches, and a large central dome. At the base, the Charminar is a bustling area known for vibrant bazaars, including the famous Laad Bazaar known for bangles and pearls.
